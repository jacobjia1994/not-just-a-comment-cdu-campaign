Why this matters

Everyday anti-Indigenous racism is a wellbeing issue.

These points explain why this site focuses on everyday comments, bystander action, support and social and emotional wellbeing.

What the evidence says

Racism affects safety, belonging and wellbeing.

Open the research point you need. The campaign message is short; the evidence sits behind it.

Systemic racism and SEWB

AIHW/Calma et al. link systemic anti-Indigenous racism with First Nations social and emotional wellbeing and mental health. Here, racism is treated as a wellbeing concern, not a minor interpersonal disagreement.

Everyday discrimination

Kairuz et al. and Thurber et al. link racism and everyday discrimination with psychological distress and wellbeing harms. Repeated jokes, assumptions and silences can have cumulative effects.

Stigma and shame

Stigma is a power-laden social process tied to exclusion. Racist comments can create shame and silence, not only offence.

Do not individualise distress

Tseris critiques anti-stigma campaigns that individualise distress. Students harmed by racism should not be told to cope better while the room stays unchanged.

Whiteness and colour-blindness

Walter and Baltra-Ulloa focus attention on whiteness, colour-blindness and meritocracy. "Special treatment" talk can protect comfort while leaving racism unnamed.

Racism at universities

AHRC Racism@Uni identifies widespread racism in Australian universities and low complaint use. Low reporting cannot be read as proof that harm did not occur.

Bystander action

Bystander research stresses recognition, responsibility, confidence and practical words. People are more likely to act when they know what harm looks like and have words to use.

Cultural safety and artwork

First Nations ethical principles and self-determination caution against decorative cultural use. Aboriginal art, stories or cultural material should not be used without permission, payment, review and attribution.

Visual and cultural safety note

Use them before group work, tutorial discussion, placement preparation, student leadership training or a difficult conversation after a harmful comment.
