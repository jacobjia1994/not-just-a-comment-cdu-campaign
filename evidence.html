Why this is built this way

Everyday racism is a wellbeing issue.

This campaign focuses on short bystander practice because students often know something is wrong but do not have the confidence, words or peer support to act.

01

Racism affects social and emotional wellbeing.

Anti-Indigenous racism is not only interpersonal rudeness. It is connected with chronic stress, psychological distress, reduced belonging and reduced participation. For First Nations peoples, this must be understood in relation to colonisation, systemic racism and social and emotional wellbeing.

02

Everyday comments are not trivial.

Jokes, stereotypes, "special treatment" myths, identity questioning and cultural-labour demands help create a climate where First Nations students may feel watched, doubted, unsafe or burdened.

03

Bystanders are a practical target.

Australian bystander anti-racism research shows that many people disapprove of racism but hesitate because they lack confidence, language, peer support or a clear sense of what action is safe.

04

Practice beats passive awareness.

The campaign uses scenarios, response choices and short scripts because anti-racism work is stronger when people rehearse behaviour, not only receive information.
