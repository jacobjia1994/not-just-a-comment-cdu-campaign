How the campaign works

A small site for moments that are too often brushed aside.

The resource is designed for CDU students who are not the target of racism but may be present when it happens. Each page moves from recognition to action, support and evidence.

01

Recognise

Common campus comments are unpacked so students can see how jokes, myths and silence create stigma and exclusion.

02

Respond

Students are given short, usable bystander lines instead of being told simply to "call it out" without support.

03

Check in

The resource emphasises private support and not making Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander students educate the room.

04

Connect

Support links, official videos and evidence sit beside the campaign so it is more than a slogan.

Social and emotional wellbeing

Racism is a wellbeing issue.

First Nations social and emotional wellbeing is holistic. It includes body, mind, family, kinship, community, culture, Country, spirituality and history. When racism affects safety, belonging or identity, it becomes a barrier to wellbeing.

This campaign does not ask First Nations students to become more resilient. It asks the room to change.

Situated in Darwin

Designed for a CDU context, not a generic awareness poster.

A real version would need First Nations review and CDU approval. This student version is situated in Darwin and recognises that CDU's learning spaces, placements, group work and online discussions are all places where everyday racism can be normalised or interrupted.

On Country

The public version avoids decorative borrowing from Aboriginal art. A real CDU campaign should commission or license artwork through First Nations artists and local cultural authority.

For bystanders

The campaign speaks mainly to non-Indigenous students because the burden of changing racist peer norms should not sit with students who are harmed by them.

For assessment

The website format allows the campaign to meet the creative resource requirement while also demonstrating rationale, audience, method and evidence.
