CDU student campaign

Don’t let it pass.

A joke. A “special treatment” comment. A question that puts one First Nations student on the spot.

Everyday anti-Indigenous racism affects safety, belonging and wellbeing at university. CDU students can notice it, name it and stop passing the burden on.

What gets passed on

It is not just one comment when the same burden keeps moving.

Everyday racism can create shame, identity stress and cultural load. It can also change how safe someone feels in class, online, on placement or on campus.

Tap the chain

Small moments can carry a heavy load.

The campaign is not about fixing First Nations students. It is about changing the behaviour around them.

Is this racism?

Racism is not always loud.

Click a moment. Each one is common enough to be excused, and harmful enough to matter.

Learn without loading others

Curiosity is not the problem. Passing the work on is.

Wanting to learn is good. But do not make a First Nations classmate become the teacher, evidence source or spokesperson for everyone.

One next move

You do not need the perfect speech.

You need one realistic action that reduces harm, shifts the burden and shows the room that racism is not being passed on.
