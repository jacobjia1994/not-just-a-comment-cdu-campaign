Everyday anti-Indigenous racism and wellbeing

Not Just a Comment

Everyday anti-Indigenous racism can affect belonging, safety and social and emotional wellbeing.

In tutorials, group work, online spaces and placement conversations, silence can teach the room that racism is acceptable.

When the room goes quiet

Small comments can carry a heavy load.

If you witness, repeat or minimise everyday anti-Indigenous racism, you can help change what the room accepts. Notice the harm, interrupt the pattern, check in and use support pathways when needed.

01

Recognise

Notice jokes, myths, colour-blind comments and cultural labour that can make racism seem ordinary.

Understand

Connect these moments to stigma, shame, belonging and social and emotional wellbeing.

03

Respond

Use short bystander lines that reduce harm without putting the person targeted on display.

04

Follow through

Check in privately, document safely, seek advice and respect the affected person's choice about reporting.

Social and emotional wellbeing

Racism is a wellbeing issue.

Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander social and emotional wellbeing includes body, mind, family, kinship, community, culture, Country, spirituality and history. Racism can harm wellbeing when it makes a person feel watched, questioned, stereotyped, isolated or unsafe in a learning space.

The room needs to change before the person harmed is asked to cope.

In CDU learning spaces

These moments can happen wherever students learn together.

Tutorials, group work, online discussions, campus life and placement conversations can all normalise racism or interrupt it. The difference often starts with one person refusing to move on as if nothing happened.

Notice place

Learning in Darwin means taking Country, history and local relationships seriously, not treating culture as decoration.

Reduce the burden

Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander students should not have to educate the room before the room takes racism seriously.

Keep learning

Use First Nations-led resources, ask better questions and take responsibility for what happens after a harmful comment.
