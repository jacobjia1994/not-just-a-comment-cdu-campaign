Five days, five moments

Everyday racism often arrives as ordinary campus talk.

These are composite educational scenarios. They are not the story of any individual student.

Day 1

"It was only a joke."

What is happening

A racist joke is treated as harmless humour. The group is invited to laugh, stay quiet or move on.

Why it matters

The joke teaches the room what is allowed. It can make identity feel like a punchline and make speaking up risky.

Try this

"Can we not make people the punchline?" Then check in privately with anyone affected.

Day 2

"They get special treatment."

What is happening

Equity support is framed as unfair advantage instead of a response to unequal conditions.

Why it matters

This can shame Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander students for using support or being present at university.

Try this

"Support programs are about equity, not unfair advantage." Keep the focus on systems, not one student.

Day 3

"I do not see race."

What is happening

Someone presents colour-blindness as fairness and avoids talking about racism directly.

Why it matters

Colour-blindness can make racism harder to name and can make people feel unheard when they identify harm.

Try this

"Treating people fairly includes noticing how racism affects people differently."

Day 4

"Can you explain the Aboriginal view?"

What is happening

One student is asked to speak for diverse peoples, histories, cultures and communities.

Why it matters

It turns a student into a cultural help desk and can make participation feel conditional on unpaid education work.

Try this

"Let's not put one person on the spot. We can use First Nations-led resources and do the reading."

Day 5

"No one reported it, so it must be fine."

What is happening

Silence or low complaint numbers are treated as evidence that racism is not happening.

Why it matters

People may not report racism when the process feels unsafe, tiring, unclear or unlikely to help.

Try this

"People do not always report racism, even when it causes harm." Check in, document safely and seek advice.
