Support and reporting

No complaint does not mean no harm.

Students may avoid reporting racism because the process feels unsafe, exhausting, unclear or unlikely to lead to change.

Start with care

Support is not only a formal complaint.

A useful response can be private, practical and low pressure: check in, listen, offer options, document safely and ask what the person wants next.

Check in

Ask what the person needs. Keep the conversation private unless they want something else.

Offer options

Offer to stay, leave, find a staff member, look up support, write down what happened or sit with them while they decide.

Do not take over

Do not force a complaint. Do not tell the story without consent. Do not turn support into pressure.

When wellbeing support is urgent

If there is immediate danger, call 000. Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people can contact 13YARN on 13 92 76 for crisis support. Lifeline is available on 13 11 14.

This student site is not a counselling or crisis service.

If you witnessed racism and feel unsure what to do, seek advice without naming the person harmed unless you have consent or there is immediate risk.
