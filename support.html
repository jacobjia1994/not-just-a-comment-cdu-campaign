If racism happens

Support matters.

You do not have to decide everything at once. Support can mean talking with someone, writing down what happened, asking about options or making a report.

01

If you experienced racism

You do not have to prove the harm was serious enough before you seek support.

  • Move to a safer space.
  • Talk with someone you trust.
  • Write down what happened.
  • Seek wellbeing or cultural support.
  • Ask about CDU support or complaint pathways.
02

If you witnessed racism

Your role is not to investigate, take over or pressure the person to report.

  • Check in.
  • Name that what happened was not okay.
  • Ask what support they want.
  • Offer to record what you saw.
  • Avoid making the person explain the harm to you.
03

If you caused harm

Do not make the person harmed manage your guilt.

  • Stop defending the comment.
  • Listen to the impact.
  • Apologise without demanding forgiveness.
  • Learn from public resources.
  • Change the behaviour.
04

If someone tells you they were harmed

Start with: “I’m sorry that happened. I believe you. What would be useful right now?”

Avoid: “Are you sure?”, “Maybe they didn’t mean it”, “Why didn’t you report it?” or “You should educate them.”
