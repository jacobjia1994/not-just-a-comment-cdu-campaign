Video room

Watch, discuss, apply.

These official public videos can support classroom discussion or tutorial reflection when each one is tied to a concrete question.

The Invisible Discriminator

Beyond Blue Official. Useful for discussing the link between racism, discrimination and mental health.

  • Notice: How small repeated comments can build pressure over time.
  • Discuss: Where does the video show wellbeing harm without using diagnostic labels?
  • Apply at CDU: What everyday comment in a tutorial or group chat could have a similar cumulative effect?

What you say matters

Australian Human Rights Commission. A direct prompt about everyday racist speech and responsibility.

  • Notice: How speech can set a peer norm.
  • Discuss: What makes a racist comment easier to dismiss as "just words"?
  • Apply at CDU: What short line could a student use before the conversation moves on?

Elevator

Australian Human Rights Commission. Useful for discussing public silence and quick bystander choices.

  • Notice: How bystanders make quick decisions under social pressure.
  • Discuss: What stops people from speaking in a small shared space?
  • Apply at CDU: What would be a low-risk response in a crowded campus or online setting?

Taxi

Australian Human Rights Commission. A short scenario for noticing how ordinary settings can license racism.

  • Notice: How public settings can make racism feel licensed.
  • Discuss: What options exist when direct confrontation may feel unsafe?
  • Apply at CDU: How could a student redirect, document or check in later?

Racism. It Stops With Me

Australian Human Rights Commission. A recognisable example for social norm messaging.

  • Notice: How public messages work best when they point toward action.
  • Discuss: What does "it stops with me" require beyond agreement?
  • Apply at CDU: What would show that a student group has changed its norm?
